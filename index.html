Introduction

Hi! You've happened upon the GAVisTool website. (I know; it doesn't look like much.) Anyhow, this site is designed to introduce you to the GAVisTool software, and show you how to use it. Some basic knowledge of geometric algebra (GA) is assumed. For more information on that, the following links come highly recommended by yours truely.

  • The University of Amsterdam GA Website -- Here you'll find tutorials on GA and CGA using the GAViewer software. After visiting this site, you may not decide to come back to this one.
  • The University of Cambridge GA Website -- The researchers at this university have written a book on GA targeted at physicists, but you don't have to one to get something out of their book.
  • Geometric Calculus R & D Home Page -- Maintained by David Hestenes himself, this site contains a great deal of information on GA and GC, though perhaps not very accessible to the average reader.
  • Professor Macdonald's Home Page -- Professor Macdonald is the author of a number of introductory undergraduate texts on the subjects of linear and geometric algebra, as well as geometric calculus.

Those are just a few sources. Googling the internet, you're bound to run into several tutorials on GA that a number of people have been kind enough to put together.

All that said, what is GAVisTool? GAVisTool is an interactive computer graphics program designed to demonstrate and explore concepts from GA and conformal geometric algebra (CGA). It provides the user with a computation environment and view of 3D Euclidean space where experiments may be rigged. The term "rig" is used here in the same sense that it is used in common modeling programs such as Maya. The following link takes you to a tutorial on how to use the software along with a list of subsequent tutorials at the end.

The GAVisTool Tutorial

Who is the intended audience for the GAVisTool program? To be honest, the only intended user of GAVisTool is myself, and it is easy to see that the program is a work in progress. Keep this in mind if you actually try to use it to learn anything about CGA or do any reserach that involves CGA. But if this is the case, why create a website with tutorials for potential users? Well, while I cannot devote the time and resources to fully maintain the software for other users, I enjoy sharing knowledge about CGA, and one way to do that is through the GAVisTool program. It would be interesting to me to hear about anyone who might have been able to benefit from the program, but again, I have written it purely as an exercise in learning CGA for myself. Additionally, the program may be of interest to prospective employers; though I sincerely hope to remain in my current vocation as long as possible.
