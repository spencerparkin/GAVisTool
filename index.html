GAVisTool

Geometric Algebra Visualization Tool

By Spencer T. Parkin

Introduction

Hi! You've happened upon the GAVisTool website. (I know; it doesn't look very impressive.) Anyhow, this site is designed to introduce you to the GAVisTool software, and show you how to use it. Some basic knowledge of geometric algebra (GA) is assumed. For more information on that, the following links come highly recommended by yours truely.

  • The University of Amsterdam GA Website -- Here you'll find tutorials on GA and CGA using the GAViewer software. After visiting this site, you may not decide to come back to this one.
  • Geometric Calculus R & D Home Page -- Maintained by David Hestenes himself, this site contains a great deal of information on GA and GC, though perhaps not very accessible to the average reader.
  • Professor Macdonald's Home Page -- Professor Macdonald is the author of a number of introductory undergraduate texts on the subjects of linear and geometric algebra, as well as geometric calculus.
  • The University of Cambridge GA Website -- The researchers at this university have written a book on GA targeted at physicists, but you don't have to one to get something out of their book.

Those are just a few sources. Googling the internet, you're bound to run into several tutorials on GA that a number of people have been kind enough to put together.

That said, what is GAVisTool? GAVisTool is an interactive computer graphics program designed to demonstrate and explore concepts from GA and conformal geometric algebra. It provides the user with a computation environment and view of 3D Euclidean space where experaments may be rigged. The term rig is used here in the same sense as it is in common modeling programs such as Maya.

Getting Started

To get started, you need the GAVisTool software installed on your computer. This is as simple as copying down the executable from here. At the time of this writing, only windows is supported. If you prefer to download the source code and compile the project, you can do that by visiting the GAVisTool project on git-hub here, and then simply clone the repository. Alternatively, you can download a ZIP file of the project from here, or a tar-ball from here.
